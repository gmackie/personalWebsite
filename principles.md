How I think about building.

**Ship, then polish.** A working thing in the world beats a perfect thing in your head. You learn more from one real user than a hundred imagined ones.

**Own the stack.** Self-hosted infrastructure means no surprise bills, no vendor lock-in, no one else's outage. The cost is maintenance. The payoff is understanding.

**Build in public.** Sharing what you're working on attracts the right people. The changelog is the resume.

**Manual first, automate second.** Prove that a process is worth doing before you spend time automating it. Most automation is premature.

**One person can do a lot.** AI makes the marginal cost of building near-zero. The bottleneck is taste and judgment, not headcount.

**Breadth is a feature.** Working across multiple domains means you see patterns others miss. The best ideas come from cross-pollination.

**Quality matters at every scale.** Bugs matter. Edge cases matter. The last 5% is where trust is built or broken.
